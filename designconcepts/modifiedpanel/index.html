Most Modified Panel Case Study

Exploring how a dynamic, user-focused panel can simplify complex parameter editing for engineers while maintaining workspace clarity and usability.

Overview

In complex CAD and engineering tools, users often manage multiple overlapping panels to tweak model parameters. This constant toggling disrupts focus and clutters the workspace.

The Most Modified Panel concept simplifies this by surfacing only the most relevant parameters — allowing engineers to edit, pin, and visualize changes in real time, without losing sight of their work.

Design Objective

To create a dynamic, customizable panel that adapts to each user’s workflow by surfacing their most-used parameters, improving efficiency and reducing cognitive load during modeling tasks.

Wireframe

Annotations

  1. Main Model Workspace: The central focus area where engineers view and manipulate their 3D models. The workspace remains fully visible throughout adjustments, reducing visual disruption and helping users maintain design context.
  2. Dockable “Most Modified” Panel: A right-hand side panel showing the most frequently edited parameters. It can be docked, resized, or hidden to support flexible workflows across different screen sizes and user preferences.
  3. Pinned Parameters: Engineers can pin or unpin specific parts, features, or dimensions based on their personal workflow needs. This feature adapts the interface dynamically to individual tasks and project phases.
  4. Real-Time Preview: As parameters are changed, updates appear instantly within the main model workspace. This immediate feedback minimizes context switching and reduces trial-and-error cycles.
  5. Advanced Settings Toggle: A collapsible section that reveals rarely used or interdependent parameters. Keeps the main panel clean and approachable while still supporting power users who require deeper control.

Outcome

By simplifying how parameters are accessed and adjusted, the Most Modified Panel concept reduces repetitive actions and interface clutter while encouraging a more fluid, immersive design flow.

This approach blends UI efficiency with UX empathy, giving engineers greater control over their creative and analytical work without sacrificing performance or clarity.
