Early Concept / Sketching

My early design inspiration came from Microsoft Word, Google Docs, and Framer. Each had strengths and weaknesses, and I wanted Quillty to declutter, organize, and feel intuitive.

Key ideas included:

  • Streamlining document tools so everything has a clear, dedicated place.
  • Starting with the basics and building up gradually, keeping flexibility for future features.
  • Modernizing the interface to feel cleaner and more approachable than many traditional tools, while exploring subtleties that often get overlooked.

Low-Fidelity Layout

The low-fidelity mockup set the stage for Quillty's structure. It established the main workspace, the document hierarchy panel on the left, and the tools panel on the right. I made deliberate choices about how users would interact with these areas to keep things intuitive. This stage also gave me early insight into how small design decisions could simplify workflows in ways that aren’t immediately obvious.

What’s the Backstory?

Quillty started one night out of frustration with the complexity of existing writing tools. Many applications do very specific things, but often at the cost of clarity and simplicity.

Most tools come with so much unnecessary "stuff," making the user experience cluttered. Highly requested features are frequently locked behind monetization models, leaving users feeling drained instead of empowered.

Writing should be simple, approachable, and human-centered. Quillty was designed to provide just the essentials, stripping away distractions and focusing on what truly matters — the act of writing itself. I’ll be using Quillty myself, keeping it practical, intuitive, and continuously refined from a real user’s perspective.

What Will It Solve?

  • Reduces the clutter and distraction common in other writing tools.
  • Delivers core functionality for free, without unnecessary monetization barriers.
  • Focuses on human-centered design to make writing intuitive and enjoyable.
  • Provides a scalable foundation for personal and professional use.

Why This One Project?

Every portfolio shows what a designer can do visually, but the story behind it is just as important. Quillty demonstrates a thoughtful approach to user experience, problem-solving, and design clarity. Even the progress shown to a small group of interested people shows that the interface choices and the reasoning behind them are steps in the right direction. This proves there is some credibility to Quillty’s approach where other writing tools fall short.
