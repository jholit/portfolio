Early Concept / Sketching

My early design inspiration came from Microsoft Word, Google Docs, and Framer. Each had strengths and weaknesses, and I wanted Quillty to declutter, organize, and feel intuitive.

Key ideas included:

  • Streamlining document tools so everything has a clear, dedicated place.
  • Starting with the basics and building up gradually, keeping flexibility for future features.
  • Modernizing the interface to feel cleaner and more approachable than many traditional tools, while exploring subtleties that often get overlooked.

Low-Fidelity Layout

The low-fidelity mockup set the stage for Quillty's structure. It established the main workspace, the document hierarchy panel on the left, and the tools panel on the right. I made deliberate choices about how users would interact with these areas to keep things intuitive. This stage also gave me early insight into how small design decisions could simplify workflows in ways that aren’t immediately obvious.

What’s the Backstory?

The idea of Quillty came one night out of frustration at having too many applications that did very specific things. We all use different software daily for various functions, both personal and professional.

Many of these come with so much unnecessary "stuff," when all we need is one simple tool. And often, highly requested features are locked behind monetization models that leave you feeling drained.

This is real for many of us in a digital age — myself included. When it comes to writing in its truest, purest, and most authentic form, why has it become a commodity when it should be free and accessible?

Writing is a basic human right, and taking it away diminishes the ability to communicate and build civilization. We deserve a simple, human-owned and centered tool. Quillty is designed to strip away distractions and provide just the essentials — making writing approachable, intuitive, and enjoyable again.

What Will It Solve?

  • Reduces the clutter and distraction common in other writing tools.
  • Provides core functionality for free, without unnecessary monetization barriers.
  • Focuses on human-centered design to make writing intuitive and enjoyable.
  • Offers a conceptual approach to word processing with scalability for personal and professional use.
