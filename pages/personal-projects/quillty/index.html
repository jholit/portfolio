UX Research Project

Quillty

Quillty is a product design exploration focused on creating a modern, human-centered writing environment that prioritizes clarity, restraint, and writer flow over feature bloat.

Work in progress
01 — Project Snapshot

Why Quillty Exists

Quillty was born out of a frustrating but inspired night of bouncing between writing tools that failed to synchronize, unified neither purpose nor workflow, and ultimately distracted from the act of writing itself.

Modern writing applications often feel bloated, monetized, and detached from the human-centered foundation of writing. Subscription models and feature sprawl have shifted focus away from expression and clarity.

Quillty challenges that trajectory by reframing writing software as an environment rather than a toolset—one that supports both professional and creative work without attempting to write for the user.

02 — Role & Constraints

Design Perspective

My role in Quillty extended beyond interface design. The project required thinking like a writer first, rather than approaching the problem from a feature-driven or corporate perspective.

Writing is inherently bottom-up: anyone can begin with words on a page, but not every environment supports focus, structure, or sustained flow. Every design decision was evaluated against a single question:

“Does this environment help the writer do what they already know how to do?”

This version of Quillty is intentionally mid-fidelity. It explores structure, hierarchy, and interaction philosophy rather than final visual polish or full prototyping.

03 — Design Philosophy

Solving the Hard Problems First

Quillty represents a broader shift in my design process. Rather than refining surfaces first, I focus on identifying and resolving the most structurally difficult problems early.

Once those foundational decisions are made, everything else—from user flows to interface layout—becomes clearer and more coherent.

The interface draws lessons from tools like Microsoft Word, Google Drive, Adobe Reader, and Framer. Each influenced Quillty in different ways, from document structure to visual restraint.

The result is an environment that remains quiet, collapsible, and focused—protecting the central writing space from unnecessary interruption.

04 — Navigation Structure

Top-Level Organization

Quillty’s navigation bar establishes the application’s primary structure without competing for attention. It anchors document identity and workspace controls while remaining visually understated.

Future iterations may expand its capabilities, but the guiding principle remains consistent: navigation should support writing, not interrupt it.

05 — Side Panels

Structure Without Distraction

Quillty is organized around two primary side panels: a Mapping Panel on the left and a Styles Panel on the right. Together, they provide structure and control while preserving focus.

Mapping Panel

The Mapping Panel organizes long-form documents into a clear hierarchy. It enables navigation between sections without breaking flow or context.

Styles Panel

The Styles Panel centralizes formatting and media tools in a minimal, scrollable environment. Its design avoids the clutter typical of traditional word processors.

A key design decision involved separating content uploads into a dedicated collapsible area beneath the panel. This prevents constant scrolling and preserves the panel’s predictability.

Editing controls balance keyboard-driven input and structured dropdowns, allowing power without visual noise.

06 — Future Direction

Evolving the Environment

Quillty currently focuses on the core writing experience. Future exploration may expand into note-taking systems, enhanced writing support, synchronization, and lightweight file management.

The guiding principle remains restraint. Features are only added when they genuinely support clarity and consistency rather than novelty.
