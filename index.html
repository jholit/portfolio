TennaJam – Dedicated Game-Server Hosting Application

A product design case study focused on making server setup and management feel straightforward, guided, and forgiving — even for people who don’t speak “DevOps,” including myself.

Click the image to view the full case study

Problem & Approach

Server tools assume deep technical knowledge and scatter actions across complex dashboards. The goal was to design a clearer, human-centered flow for:

  • Creating and naming new servers
  • Choosing regions and core settings confidently
  • Surfacing advanced options without overwhelming
  • Managing servers from a simple, structured overview

This became an end-to-end flow design problem: mapping intent, placing decisions logically, and designing a UI that keeps users oriented start-to-finish.

What I Focused On

  • Clarifying setup steps and labels
  • Defining a “Server Management” hub
  • Designing low-fi wireframes for key screens
  • Balancing flexibility with sensible defaults
  • Documenting design trade-offs and rationale

The full case study explores the hosting flow, management dashboard, and details view — each designed for clarity and future scalability.

About
