Bookmark Side Panel - Improving Structure Hierarchy, and Scan Speed

Product Design

A product design exploration focused on improving how users view, scan, and organize bookmarks through clearer structure and reduced visual noise.

  • Clear category and subfolder hierarchy
  • Faster visual scanning and recognition
  • Reduced eye strain through softer visual emphasis
  • Improved structural clarity without adding complexity

Bookmark panels are used constantly but often feel cluttered or fatiguing over time. This project refines the panel’s hierarchy and visual balance, making saved items easier to recognize and manage while preserving familiarity and user control.

ChatGPT — Redesigning Interface Clarity, Conversation, and Navigation Roles

Product Design

A conversation-first interface redesign focused on reducing cognitive friction by separating navigation from communication and preserving the conversation as the primary workspace.

  • Distraction-minimal conversation space
  • Clear separation of navigation and communication
  • Predictable, consistent interface structure
  • Foundational layout for future feature scalability

Many conversational tools expose system structure in ways that interrupt flow and increase mental overhead. This redesign focuses on preserving uninterrupted conversation by aligning the interface with how users naturally think, allowing communication to remain continuous, focused, and free from structural friction.

Homepage Redesign – Renegade Game Studios

An Audit in Low-Fidelity Webpage Restructuring

A redesign of the Renegade Game Studios homepage, addressing clarity issues caused by competing priorities and unclear content hierarchy. It's current iteration tries to do too much at once, which makes it harder for users to quickly understand what the site is actually for. Even with brand familiarity, the current experience makes it difficult to quickly understand intent and next steps.

This redesigned layout is about getting back to the basics — reorienting the homepage around clarity, confidence, and intent. The goal is to improve UI/UX at the root, reduce friction, and increase user retention, session depth, and engagement by making it immediately clear "why" someone is there and what they can do next. About

Me, The Product Designer

I design products by focusing on system behavior, interaction clarity, and how people move through applications without unnecessary complexity. Clear structure, predictable behavior, and usability guide every decision. My goal is to reduce cognitive friction and create interfaces that feel intuitive, calm, and easy to use.

How I Work

  • Map user flows before designing visuals

  • Refine layouts with the full journey in mind
  • Document decisions, reasoning, and trade-offs
